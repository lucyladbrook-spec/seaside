The Rock Pool

What is a rock pool?

A small pool of seawater left between rocks when the tide goes out.

Look closely — spot these
crab seaweed mussels limpet anemone
Key words
tide habitat creature
Did you know?

Rock pools are like tiny hidden worlds. Hundreds of creatures can live in just one pool! When the tide comes back in, the creatures are covered with fresh seawater again.

The Cliff

What is a cliff?

A steep rock face beside the sea.

Look closely — spot these
rocks sea birds wildflowers waves
Key words
erosion steep coastal
Did you know?

The sea slowly wears away cliffs over thousands of years — this is called erosion. The famous White Cliffs of Dover are made of chalk!

Stay safe!

Always stay well back from cliff edges. The ground can crumble without any warning.

Talk partners

"Why must we always stay away from cliff edges?"

A sheltered place where boats can stay safely.

Key words

Did you know?

St Ives in Cornwall has a beautiful harbour. Fishermen have used it for hundreds of years to land their catch safely after a long trip out to sea.

The Port

What is a port?

A place where ships load and unload goods or passengers.

Look closely — spot these
large ships cranes containers warehouses lorries
Key words
cargo import export
Did you know?

The Port of Felixstowe in Suffolk is the UK's biggest port. Huge ships bring bananas, electronics and cars from all over the world!

The Pier

What is a pier?

A structure built out into the sea, so you can walk above the waves.

Look closely — spot these
wooden legs people walking waves underneath flags ice cream stall
Key words
structure Victorian promenade
Did you know?

Brighton Pier is over 500 metres long! Piers were built in Victorian times so families could stroll out over the sea for a breath of fresh air.

The Promenade

What is a promenade?

A path beside the beach where people can walk and enjoy the sea air.

Look closely — spot these
benches railings lamp posts beach huts bunting
Key words
seafront railings leisure
Did you know?

The word "promenade" comes from French and means a leisurely walk. Victorian families dressed in their finest clothes to stroll along the prom!

The Lighthouse

What is a lighthouse?

A tall building with a bright flashing light to guide ships safely past rocks.

Look closely — spot these
light stripes rocks sea seagulls
Key words
beacon warning navigation
Did you know?

Lighthouse keepers used to live inside lighthouses and wind up the light by hand every night! Today most lighthouses are controlled automatically.

Coastal Wildlife

The seaside is home to some wonderful creatures. How many can you spot?

Seagull
Clever birds that will steal your chips! They nest on cliffs and rooftops.
Cliffs, beaches, harbours
Oystercatcher
This bold black-and-white bird has a bright orange beak — perfect for prising open mussels!
Rocky shores, beaches
Seal
Grey seals love to bask on rocks. They can hold their breath for up to 30 minutes underwater!
Rocky coasts, beaches
Crab
Crabs walk sideways and can regrow a lost claw. They hide under rocks at low tide.
Rock pools, beaches
Jellyfish
Jellyfish have no brain, no heart and no eyes — but they have lived on Earth for over 500 million years!
In the sea, washed on beaches
Mussels
Mussels cling to rocks in large clusters. They filter seawater to find their food.
Rocks, rock pools
Shells
Shells are the hard homes of sea creatures. Each type of shell belongs to a different animal.
Beaches, rock pools
Seaweed
Seaweed is food and shelter for many sea creatures. People even use it to make food and medicines!
Rock pools, beaches, rocks
